Directed by : Marcin Wrona

Language : Polish

Year : 2015

Country : Poland

Genre : Psychological Horror, Dark Comedy, Drama, Thriller

Starring : Ryan Gosling, Harrison Ford, Ana de Armas, Sylvia Hoeks, Robin Wright, Mackenzie Davis, Dave Bautista, Jared Leto

Synopsis : Piotr (Itay Tiran) receives a piece of land as a gift for his upcoming wedding. While preparing the property to build a home for his new family, he finds human remains in the ground. He decides to keep the discovery to himself so that all of the wedding arrangements can go on as planned. However, the deeper he tries to keep his secret, the more he seems to be under control of something — or rather someone — and from then on very strange things begin to happen.

My View :

Demon is a Polish horror movie, directed by Marcin Wrona, starrind Itay Tiran a a groom possessed by a dybbuk (a clinging spirit, according to Jewish mythology) during his wedding eve. This is the final movie of Marcin Wrona who committed suicide while the movie was doing its rounds in film festivals. As his final movie, Marcin Wrona delivered a haunting, bone chilling, atmospheric horror movie about the ghosts of Poland, which, like one of the characters say, “The whole country’s built on corpses”.

Coming to the actors’ performances, all main actors gave strong performances, especially Itay Tiran, who gave an awesome performance as Piotr, one of the most tormented grooms in cinema history. he effectively brought in changes in the groom’s personality gradually as the movie progresses. Agnieszka Zulewska as Zaneta, the bride trying to find out what happened with her fiance, Andrzej Grabowski as the bride’s father, and Tomasz Schuchardt as the bride’s brother and the groom’s friend who is also very eager to please his father, gave great performances. The technical aspects of the movie were also great, the haunting music, gorgeous visuals, and editing were all awesome. The movie has also got some well written characters like the groom, bride, bride’s brother, bride’s father, a jewish professor, a priest who says he’ll only believe what he sees, and a doctor who claims himself to be an atheist. Marcin Wrona compiled all these perfectly to give one of the most haunting horror movie experience in recent times.

The movie gives a glimpse into the past political landscape of Poland through the characters, Hana (Maria Debska), Zaneta’s father (Andrzej Grabowski), and a Jewish teacher (Włodzimierz Press), and how some poles usurped Jewish property left behind by the Jews during the second world war. The movie also shows how a society selectively erases details about their dark past. This movie was also subjected to vote brigading (collectively giving low ratings to a movie by a group of people, in this case about 1000 people, if online articles are to be believed) in IMDb, which means the movie will not be shown in any of the IMDb great movies list.

The movie is made as a ‘slow burn’ horror movie, building up the tension, instead of relying in not-so-scary jump scares to give chills to the viewer. Some scenes will genuinely give you the creeps, like the scene with a jewish professor where he identifies the spirit and the scene where a doctor attending to Piotr (Itay Tiran), starts to realise what’s actually going on. Unlike most recent horror movies, in this movie, the possession does not give the possessed any kind of superhuman powers. The movie has got an open ending, leaving the viewers to introspect and find out what really happened from what they’ve seen, it could be really unsettling for some viewers.

Demon is an intelligent and thought provoking movie, with awesome performances from the cast members and you can also feel the great work done by the crew members behind the camera as well. Marcin Wrona delivered a haunting, slow burn, well made psychodrama, horror movie, which would become more haunting after knowing that Marcin Wrona committed suicide some time after making this movie. Personally, Demon is one of the best horror movie experiences of recent times, for me. Please let me know of your opinon about the movie, if you’ve watched it, through the comments section.

COD Rating : 5/5
